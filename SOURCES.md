## Primary Historical Sources

### Emakimono (Picture Scrolls)

- *Tsukumogami Emaki* (付喪神絵巻) — Muromachi period scroll depicting animated objects
- *Hyakki Yagyo Emaki* (百鬼夜行絵巻) — Various scrolls showing "Night Parade of 100 Demons"
- *Bakemono Emaki* (化物絵巻) — Monster scrolls from Edo period

### Edo Period Art

- Toriyama Sekien (鳥山石燕). *Gazu Hyakki Yagyō* (画図百鬼夜行, "The Illustrated Night Parade of Hundred Demons"), 1776
- Toriyama Sekien. *Konjaku Gazu Zoku Hyakki* (今昔画図続百鬼, "The Illustrated One Hundred Demons from the Present and the Past"), 1779
- Various ukiyo-e prints by Utagawa Kuniyoshi, Katsushika Hokusai

## Cultural Practices & Ceremonies

### Kuyo Ceremonies

- Hari Kuyo (針供養) — Needle memorial services at temples nationwide
  - Major locations: Awashima Shrine (Wakayama), Sensoji Temple (Tokyo)
- Ningyo Kuyo (人形供養) — Doll memorial services
- Fude Kuyo (筆供養) — Brush memorial services
